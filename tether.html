Tether (USDT)

Origin



Tether was founded in July 2014 by a company called Tether Limited. It originally known as "Realcoin". It also called as "stablecoin" because it aims to keep a 1:1 exchange ratio with U.S. dollar. The first tokens were issued on 6 October 2014, using the Omni Layer Protocol on the Bitcoin blockchain.

Brief History


Launch of Tether (2014) :

Tether was founded in 2014 by a company called Tether Limited. Tether was launched with objective of creating a stablecoin that could provide stability in the volatile cryptocurrency market by pegging its value to real-world assets, primarily the US dollar.

New Owner (2015) :

In 2015, Tether was purchased by new owners based in Hong Kong, who also owned the Bitfinex crypto centralized exchange, and the subsequent integration of the token into the Bitfinex ecosystem increased its popularity.

Controversies and Scrutiny (2017-2019) :

Tether faced various controversies during this period. Concerns were raised about the transparency of its reserves and allegations of unbacked issuance. Critics questioned whether Tether Limited actually held enough US dollars to cover all the USDT tokens in circulation. These controversies led to regulatory scrutiny, including investigations by the U.S. Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) and the Department of Justice (DOJ).

Continued Popularity (2020-Now) :

Despite the controversies and regulatory scrutiny, Tether continued to be widely used in the cryptocurrency market. It expanded beyond the Bitcoin blockchain to other blockchain platforms, such as Ethereum (USDT-ETH), Tron (USDT-TRX), and more.
