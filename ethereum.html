Ethereum (ETH)

Origin

Ethereum was conceived in 2013 by programmer Vitalik Buterin. Additional founders of Ethereum included Gavin Wood, Charles Hoskinson, Anthony Di Iorio and Joseph Lubin. In 2014, development work began and was crowdfunded, and the network went live on 30 July 2015.

Brief History

Founding (2013-2014) : Ethereum was initially described in late 2013 in a white paper by Vitalik Buterin, a programmer and co-founder of Bitcoin Magazine, that described a way to build decentralized applications. In 2014, to build Ethereum, they needed money. So, they asked people to invest, and they successfully raised money over $18 million.

Testing and Start (2015) : Before they officially started, they tested to make sure everything worked okay. Ethereum's mainnet officially went live on July 30, 2015, with the mining of its genesis block, known as "Block 0."

The DAO hack (2016) : A project that launched in 2016 called decentralized autonomous organization (DAO). The DAO attracted about $150 million worth of ETH in 2016, given ETH's United States dollar price at the time. However,Dow suffered a hack that took over 3.6 million ETH from the DAO's asset pool. To fix it, they did something called a "hard fork," which led to Ethereum (ETH) and Ethereum Classic (ETC) being separate.

Big Price Rise (2017) : Ethereum's price went up a lot in 2017, making it more popular. It reach nearly $1,400 USD per Ether (ETH) in December.

Upgrades (2018-Present) : Ethereum kept making changes to become better and safer. They did things like Byzantium, Constantinople, and started Ethereum 2.0 to make it faster and greener.
