Dogecoin (DOGE)

Origin



Dogecoin originally started as a "joke" which was created by IBM software engineer Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer. They created the satirical cryptocurrency to mock grandiose claims by Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies. But with Reddit's support, Dogecoin quickly gained popularity. They created a dedicated blog and forum within two weeks. Its market value peaked at $8 million, making it the seventh-largest cryptocurrency globally.

Brief History


Creation (2013) :

Dogecoin was created in December 2013 by software engineers Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer as a satirical cryptocurrency. Dogecoin was officially launched on 6 Desember 2013.

Dogewallet Hack (2013) :

In December 2013, there was a major security breach in which millions of Dogecoin were stolen from the online cryptocurrency wallet platform called Dogewallet. The Dogecoin community initiated "SaveDogemas" to donate coins to those who lost funds in the Dogewallet breach.

Rapid Grown (2013-2014) :

Within the first 30 days after it launched, there were over a million visitors to Dogecoin.com. With Reddit's support, Dogecoin quickly gained popularity in late 2013. Fron 2013 to 2014, Dogecoin's market value surged to $8 million, briefly making it the seventh-largest cryptocurrency in the world.

Community and Charitable Initiatives (2014-Now) :

In 2014, the Dogecoin community donated 27 million Dogecoins worth approximately $30,000 to fund the Jamaican bobsled team's trip to the Sochi Winter Olympic games, $11,000 worth of Dogecoin to build a well in Kenya, and $55,000 of Dogecoin to sponsor NASCAR driver Josh Wise. In 2021, Dogecoin's price also faced a big increase and subsequent fall.
