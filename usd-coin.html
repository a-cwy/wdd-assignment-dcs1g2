USD Coin (USDC)

Origin



It was launched in September 2018. It was created through a collaboration between two companies called Circle and Coinbase. The aim was to offer a trustworthy and fully-backed digital dollar, backed by a one-to-one reserve of actual US dollars held in regulated financial institutions.

Brief History


Announced and Launched (2018) :

USDC was first announced on 15 May 2018 , and was launched by Circle and Coinbase as a stablecoin in September 2018.

Blockchain Expansions (2020) :

USDC expanded beyond the Ethereum blockchain to other blockchain networks to increase accessibility and reduce transaction costs. It became available on various blockchains, including Algorand, Solana, Stellar, and more.

Visa Adopts USDC for Payments (2021) :

Visa announced that it would allow the use of USDC to settle transactions on its payment network.

Circle Takes Full Control of USDC (2023) :

In August 2023, Circle and Coinbase closed the Centre Consortium, the organization that had managed USDC since 2018, giving Circle sole governance of USDC.
